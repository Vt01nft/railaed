====================================================================
1. DESCRIPTION
====================================================================
RailAED is a UAE-first stablecoin remittance and global-payroll app built on
Circle's Arc testnet. A sender pays in AED; the recipient receives USDC in about
two seconds, with fees and finality also denominated in USDC (Arc pays gas in
USDC). Every quote is shown next to a live "honesty score" that compares what
the recipient would actually get from Al Ansari, LuLu, Wise, Western Union and
Remitly for the same AED amount.

Each recipient and each payroll contractor gets their own auto-provisioned
Circle Developer-Controlled Wallet on Arc, claimable through a WhatsApp link -
no app install, no seed phrase, no hex addresses for the end user. Every wallet
and every transfer is a real address and a real transaction, auditable on
ArcScan.

Payroll runs two ways: a one-click monthly batch (parallel USDC transfers to N
contractors), and a per-second streaming mode where salary accrues live and
settles on-chain on a short interval - producing dozens of real on-chain
transactions per minute, the exact workload Circle Nanopayments is designed to
batch gas-free in production.


====================================================================
2. PROBLEM & WHY IT MATTERS
====================================================================
Dubai is the second-largest remittance-sending city in the world. UAE expats
send billions every year to India, Pakistan, the Philippines, Egypt, Bangladesh,
Sri Lanka and Nepal - and pay roughly 1.6%-3.7% in opaque, layered FX margins to
do it, with slow settlement. The major stablecoin remittance products today
target US / LATAM / Africa corridors. Nobody is building an end-to-end
UAE-expat-outbound stablecoin product. That is the wedge RailAED takes, on the
rails Circle and Arc actually shipped.


====================================================================
3. CIRCLE PRODUCTS USED
====================================================================
- USDC on Arc
    Sub-second finality and USDC-denominated gas mean the sender's AED quote can
    be locked end-to-end with no "tx pending because gas spiked" failure mode.

- Circle Developer-Controlled Wallets
    Auto-provision a dedicated wallet per recipient and per contractor; senders
    never explain seed phrases. A passkey/email reveal path (User-Controlled
    Wallets) is mapped for v2.

- Circle Gateway / treasury (owner-wallet) pattern
    A single treasury operator wallet fans out to recipients - the operational
    shape a remittance back-office needs.

- Nanopayments-style streaming payroll (BUILT on Arc today)
    Per-second salary accrual with real on-chain settlement on each tick.
    Production swaps the per-tick transfer for Circle Nanopayments (gas-free,
    sub-cent, batched EIP-3009 settlement). The per-tick transfer is the honest
    testnet stand-in for that batching.

- StableFX (client seam BUILT; live access requested -- gated)
    The AED->USDC FX leg flows through a `StableFXClient` interface. Today it is
    `MockStableFXClient` (a live FX oracle, labelled "simulated" in the UI so the
    rail is never misrepresented). `LiveStableFXClient` drops in behind the
    `STABLEFX_ENABLED` flag the moment access + AED pair coverage exist. We
    emailed Circle Customer Care during the sprint; they confirmed testnet
    allowlisting is gated to a select group of developers and our submission did
    not qualify at this stage, so we ship the seam (the hackathon brief does not
    penalise architecture-level integrations when access is unavailable).

4. WORKING MVP - WHAT YOU CAN DO RIGHT NOW (https://railaed-uae.vercel.app)
====================================================================
- Get a quote: enter an AED amount, pick a corridor, see the recipient's USDC +
  local-currency amount and the live honesty score vs 5 traditional rails.
- Sign in with an email: provisions a Circle wallet on Arc scoped to that email;
  one-tap faucet drips 5 testnet USDC from the treasury.
- Send a remittance: provisions a recipient wallet, transfers USDC on Arc
  (~3 s), and produces an HMAC-signed WhatsApp claim link.
- Claim flow: recipient opens the link, sees their on-chain balance (read live
  from Arc) and a link to ArcScan. No install, no seed phrase.
- Payroll (batch): one click pays N contractors in parallel, each into their own
  Circle wallet, all settled in seconds.
- Payroll (streaming): per-second accrual; each tick settles on-chain on Arc.
  A one-minute run produces dozens of verifiable on-chain transactions.
- Health/audit: /api/health reports Arc chain id + block, USDC decimals, wallet
  balances, and Circle wallet-set status.

6. KEY FEATURES / DIFFERENTIATORS
====================================================================
- Live honesty score on every quote (5 traditional UAE rails, delta in USD).
- WhatsApp-native claim links - recipients never install an app or see hex.
- Per-recipient & per-contractor Circle wallets, not a shared escrow pool;
  every address is auditable on ArcScan.
- Per-user wallets with one-tap self-fund from the treasury.
- Streaming payroll: per-second pay with real on-chain settlement (the "wow"
  demo - dozens of on-chain txs per minute; Nanopayments is the production swap).
- Honest engineering: the FX leg is labelled "simulated" until the live StableFX
  rail is granted; nothing is misrepresented to the user or the judges.

8. CIRCLE PRODUCT FEEDBACK
====================================================================
What worked well:
- The @circle-fin/developer-controlled-wallets SDK is typed end-to-end; the
  createWallet -> createTransaction -> getTransaction loop is exactly three calls.
- Arc testnet RPC was reliable: INITIATED -> COMPLETE in ~3 s in every test.
- USDC-as-gas removed an entire failure mode (no separate native-token faucet).
- Per-wallet refId let us namespace wallets (railaed:transfer:<uuid>, etc.)
  without a separate join table.

What could be improved:
1. Surface real validation errors. createWallets / createTransaction failures
   return "API parameter invalid" (code 2, status 400) with no message field; we
   had to bisect (SCA account type is rejected on Arc; a tokenAddress transfer
   needs an explicit blockchain; long metadata name is rejected). A details/
   message field would have saved time.
2. Document Arc wallet account-type compatibility - SCA is in the SDK union type
   but rejected at runtime on Arc; EOA is the only valid choice today.
3. Faucet permissions - requestTestnetTokens returned 403 against our owner
   wallet; we had to bootstrap by transferring USDC from a separately-funded EOA.
4. USDC decimals on Arc - several docs/posts say 18 decimals; on-chain decimals()
   returned 6 (same as USDC elsewhere). We discovered this empirically.
5. SDK enum overlap - TestnetBlockchain vs Blockchain both include ARC-TESTNET;
   transfers use TokenBlockchain, wallet creation uses Blockchain. Easy foot-gun.
6. StableFX vs USYC access channel - the prescribed support subject line
   ("USYC or StableFX testnet request") meant our StableFX ask was handled as a
   USYC allowlisting request and declined with a USYC template; the StableFX part
   was never addressed separately. A dedicated StableFX access path would help.
